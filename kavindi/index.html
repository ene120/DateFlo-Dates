🌅 Plan 1

Golden Hour

Laid-back OB vibes, bold food, and a sunset you’ll remember

Date
Monday, March 30, 2026
Duration
~5 hours
Area
Ocean Beach + Sunset Cliffs

🌹 The Vibe

This one takes you to one of San Diego’s most laid-back neighborhoods for bold Vietnamese food at a local institution, the #1 ranked ice cream spot in the country, a walk along the OB Pier, and then a golden-hour sunset on the cliffs. It feels thoughtful and well-timed without being overproduced — and you end the night watching the sun drop into the Pacific together.

🚗 Route: Mira Mesa → OB Noodle House → An’s Gelato → OB Pier → Sunset Cliffs → Home

☔️ Weather — Monday, March 30

Expect a pleasant mid-60s afternoon cooling to upper 50s by sunset. Ocean breezes pick up at the coast in the evening — bring a light jacket or hoodie. Rain is unlikely in late March, but check the forecast a few days before.

1

Late Lunch at OB Noodle House

2218 Cable St, Ocean Beach

(619) 450-6868

3:00 – 4:15 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps Call Menu
local legend • bold flavors • happy hour

This is the real deal — a genuine OB institution featured on Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives. The pho, fried rice, and crispy egg noodles are all boldly flavored and shareable. You arrive right at 3 PM when happy hour kicks in (half-off select beers, $4 premium wells until 6 PM). Walk-in friendly, no reservation needed.

Good move: Split an appetizer, get two different entrees, and try each other’s. Pho and the Saigon fried rice are standouts. Avoid the papaya salad items (pork belly tacos, chicken satay, summer shrimp) — everything else is safe.

Monday hours: 12 PM – 11 PM.

~10 min walk to next stop
2

Dessert at An’s Dry Cleaning

1861 Bacon St, Ocean Beach

(619) 310-5028

4:30 – 5:00 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps Call
quirky • award-winning • unforgettable

Voted the #1 Ice Cream Shop in America by USA Today. This place is named after the business it replaced (a cell phone repair shop — the original location was literally called “An’s Dry Cleaning”). Every flavor has a techy name like Walkman, Bluetooth, and Motherboard. It is genuinely one-of-a-kind, and it is the kind of spot that makes a date feel like a discovery, not just a plan.

Monday hours: 12 PM – 10 PM.

~5 min walk to next stop
3

Walk the OB Pier

Niagara Ave & Abbott St, Ocean Beach

5:05 – 5:30 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps
breezy • scenic • easy transition

The longest concrete pier on the West Coast. Walk out, feel the ocean breeze, watch the surfers. This is an easy, no-pressure transition between dessert and the sunset spot. It gives you a few quiet minutes to just be together before the main event.

Free. No time limit. Just stroll.

~3 min drive to Sunset Cliffs (or ~15 min walk along the coast)
4

Sunset at Sunset Cliffs

Sunset Cliffs Blvd, San Diego

5:45 – 7:15 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps
golden hour • dramatic cliffs • the whole reason you came

This is the closer. Dramatic cliff formations, coastal arches, and unobstructed Pacific views. The trail is easy and mostly flat — no strenuous hiking, just a calm walk along the bluffs. Arrive around 5:45 to explore, find a spot to sit, and settle in for golden hour. Sunset is at 7:07 PM.

Bring: A blanket or towel to sit on. A light jacket for the ocean breeze. Snacks if you want them.

Parking: Free dirt lots off Ladera St and Cornish Dr. Also free street parking along Sunset Cliffs Blvd. Arrive before 6 PM on a nice day — spots fill up closer to sunset.

Note: No restrooms (only portable toilets near parking). Plan accordingly before driving over.

🔄 Smooth Swaps

If OB Noodle House is packed: Walk to The Joint OB at 4902 Newport Ave — great sushi and tapas, happy hour daily 12–6 PM. Same OB vibe, still within budget.

If An’s Gelato has a long line: Hit Lighthouse Ice Cream at 5059 Newport Ave — family-owned, gourmet scoops, open Mon 12–8 PM.

If weather turns cloudy: Skip the pier, drive straight to Sunset Cliffs and cozy up for a moody sunset instead. Still beautiful.

🌊 Plan 2

Coastal Charm

Sea breezes, seals, great food, and a sunset at the cove

Date
Monday, March 30, 2026
Duration
~5 hours
Area
La Jolla Village

🌹 The Vibe

This one is all about the coast. You start with a scenic cliffside trail and seal watching, then stroll through one of San Diego’s most charming villages for authentic Thai food and the best gelato in the city. The whole evening builds toward sunset at Scripps Park overlooking La Jolla Cove. Everything is walkable once you park — no car needed until it is time to go home.

🚶 Route: Mira Mesa → Coast Walk → Seals → Thai Lunch → Gelato → Sunset at the Cove → Home

☔️ Weather — Monday, March 30

Mid-60s and pleasant through the afternoon, cooling to upper 50s by sunset. La Jolla gets a steady ocean breeze — a light layer will feel great by evening. Sunscreen recommended for the Coast Walk.

1

Coast Walk Trail & Seal Watching

La Jolla Cove & Children’s Pool

2:30 – 3:30 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps
scenic • easy • opens the date with awe

The Coast Walk Trail is a 0.6-mile cliffside path with nonstop ocean views, sea lion sounds, and kayakers below. It is flat, easy, and the opposite of strenuous. After the trail, walk over to Children’s Pool to watch harbor seals sunbathing on the beach from the seawall above.

Free. No ticket, no time limit. Just show up and walk.

~10 min stroll through La Jolla Village to the restaurant
2

Late Lunch at King of Thai Cuisine

7734 Girard Ave, La Jolla

(858) 456-9044

4:00 – 5:00 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps Call Menu
authentic Thai • bold flavors • Girard Ave charm

Authentic Thai right on La Jolla’s main street. The Pad Thai, Thai Mango Chicken, and curries are all boldly flavored — nothing bland here. Dog-friendly, fresh ingredients, and the kind of local spot you would not find on your own.

Timing note: They close for a break 3–4 PM on Mondays, then reopen at 4 PM. That is why you stroll the village after the Coast Walk and arrive right when they reopen. The walk from the cove through Prospect St and Girard Ave is beautiful — browse the shops, take photos, enjoy the village.

Monday hours: 11 AM – 3 PM, 4 PM – 9 PM.

~2 min walk to next stop (same street)
3

Gelato at Bobboi Natural Gelato

8008 Girard Ave, La Jolla

5:05 – 5:25 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps
best gelato in SD • ocean-view patio • sweet transition

Voted best gelato in San Diego. Handcrafted daily on-site from local, seasonal, organic ingredients. The patio has views overlooking La Jolla Cove — so you are eating world-class gelato while looking at the Pacific. That is a pretty good moment.

Monday hours: 11 AM – 9:30 PM.

~8 min walk to sunset spot
4

Sunset at Scripps Park

1100 Coast Blvd, La Jolla

5:30 – 7:15 PM
Google Maps Apple Maps
grassy bluffs • cove views • golden light

A 5.6-acre park on rocky bluffs right next to La Jolla Cove. Find a spot on the grass, sit together, and watch the sky light up. The green belvedere huts are great for photos. Sunset is at 7:07 PM — you have almost two hours to just be here, no rush, no agenda. This is the kind of quality time that makes a date stick.

Free. Open space, ocean sounds, and nowhere you need to be.

🔄 Smooth Swaps

If King of Thai has a wait: Walk to Pho La Jolla at 3211 Holiday Ct — about a 10 min drive toward UTC. Great Vietnamese pho, open straight through (no break), very affordable.

If Bobboi is closed or packed: Grab boba at Happy Lemon in Westfield UTC — about a 10 min drive. Or keep strolling Girard Ave and grab coffee instead.

If Coast Walk feels short: Add the Scripps Coastal Meander Trail near Birch Aquarium for more cliffside ocean views. Same easy, flat walking.
